The mood board establishes a confident, culturally layered aesthetic that sits at the crossroads of sportswear heritage, European street leisure, relaxed social realism, and high-energy night culture. Even the inanimate images (the flat lays, the crate coffees, the Seine picnic) speak the same language: quiet taste, analogue warmth, the pleasure of simple things done well. ### Props and Objects

Props in the mood board function as incidental scene evidence, not featured objects. They establish that a life is being lived in the space — they are not the point of the image, and they should not be rendered as such. When generating images, treat any props as background detail: present if the scene requires it, subordinate to the human subject, never perfectly composed or lit as if for a product shot.

**What the mood board shows in passing:** vintage bicycles with leather saddles, ceramic coffee cups, folded newspapers, paperback books, analogue film cameras held loosely, wine glasses, unlabelled bottles, plastic crates used as improvised tables, a chess board set up on a crate, an iPod, a packed suitcase, food on paper on stone. These objects appear because real people have them nearby — not because a prop stylist arranged them. In generated images, none of these should be the focal point, precisely composed, or rendered with more detail than the scene's human subject warrants.

**The risk to avoid:** AI image generators tend to over-render props when they are named in a prompt. A "ceramic espresso cup" becomes a hero-lit product shot. A "chess board" becomes a symmetrically perfect centerpiece. Suppress this tendency by framing props as ambient scene context — slightly out of focus if not in the foreground, not perfectly centered, not hyper-detailed.

## Art Style and Rendering Cues

- **Photography style range:** Three distinct modes coexist — (1) candid/street that feels caught rather than staged, (2) lightly art-directed editorial that retains the quality of a real place and real moment, and (3) flat lay / still life that is quiet, object-led, and catalog-adjacent. All three are present and all belong. The mood board skews heavily toward the candid and lightly-directed end; over-composed, art-directed precision is the exception, not the rule.
- **Film grain and analogue texture:** Visible grain throughout — particularly in the street, cafe, and riverside images. The reference feel is 35mm film (Kodak Portra 400 or Fuji 400H) or a well-scanned medium-format negative. Grain is present even in the cleaner flat lay images. Digital-clean, noise-free rendering is off-brand.
- **Imperfection as authenticity:** The mood board images are not technically perfect. Highlights occasionally blow out slightly (the Seine picnic). Subjects are sometimes partially out of frame. Backgrounds are readable but not hyper-sharp. These imperfections are not failures — they are the quality. Generated images should carry the same sense that the photograph was taken, not constructed.
- **Color grading:** Warm, slightly desaturated shadows across most images; lifted highlights; green channel subdued in favor of amber/gold in interior shots; exterior street shots lean slightly cooler and more neutral. The golden-hour tailgate image has the least graded, most documentary feel — almost unprocessed.
- **Typography integration:** Overlaid text (serif headline, condensed bold sans-serif, script subheading) as a design layer — consistent with catalog or lookbook format across multiple source images
- **Realism level:** Documentary photorealism — no illustration, no CGI affect. The target is indistinguishable from a real photograph taken by a real photographer. Not "hyperrealistic" in the AI render sense — more "you could find this on a photographer's film roll."

## Anti-AI Rendering Guidelines

The following guidelines exist to counteract the specific failure modes of AI image generation. Every point describes something that AI tools do by default that the Bear Nordic aesthetic explicitly rejects. These should be treated as hard constraints when writing or evaluating prompts.

### Do Not Use
- "8K", "8K resolution", "hyperrealistic", "photorealistic rendering", "ultra-detailed", "sharp focus on every surface", "professional photography", "studio lighting"
- Symmetrical or perfectly centered compositions — AI defaults to this; push against it with off-center framing, negative space on one side, partial cropping
- Perfect simultaneous focus on all elements — if the subject is sharp, the background should breathe; if a foreground prop is sharp, the subject mid-ground may have slight softness
- Studio lighting setups — no key/fill/rim three-point lighting structures; available light only (window light, practical lamp in frame, overcast sky, tungsten spill)
- Overly styled prop arrangements — no product-shot precision; objects should look placed, not arranged

### What to Use Instead
- "35mm film", "shot on Kodak Portra 400", "Fuji 400H", "medium format film scan", "candid editorial photography"
- "available light", "natural window light", "practical tungsten", "overcast daylight", "golden hour ambient"
- "slight grain", "natural vignette", "soft focus edges", "gentle lens breathing", "slight highlight rolloff"
- "candid", "unposed", "caught mid-moment", "photographer found this scene", "unstaged"
- Compositional imperfection: "slightly off-center", "subject partially cropped at frame edge", "background slightly out of focus but readable", "foreground element partially in frame"

### The Test
Before finalizing a prompt, ask: could this image have been taken by a real photographer on a real day? If the answer is "it looks too perfect to be real," the prompt needs more imperfection language and less technical precision language.

## Models & Casting

### Age Range
The subjects across the mood board span approximately 18 to 35 years old. The core sweet spot is early-to-mid twenties — post-student, pre-settled, fully formed in taste. There is no one over 40 visible; no one who reads as a teenager. The casting suggests a generation that grew up with both the internet and analogue football culture simultaneously.

### Build and Physicality
- **Men:** Lean to athletic, with visible height in most cases. Not gym-built or overtly muscular — more the physique of someone who plays five-a-side once a week, cycles to the cafe, and eats well. Several subjects appear tall (6ft+) with long limbs that make wide-leg silhouettes look natural and purposeful. One or two subjects are more compact and stocky but styled with equal confidence.
- **Women:** Slim to medium build. Not model-industry thin — more the kind of natural slenderness that comes from an active but unbothered life. Posture tends toward relaxed rather than pulled-up; they take up space without performing it.

### Ethnicity and Diversity
The casting is genuinely and intentionally diverse without being tokenistic. Black British, West African, South Asian, East Asian, White European, and mixed-heritage individuals all appear across the mood board. No single ethnicity dominates. In the original images, Black men were central and prominent subjects; the expanded set maintains this while adding significantly more European (French, Scandinavian, Eastern European) and mixed-heritage casting. The ethnic range is wide enough that no single "default face" exists for this brand.

### Grooming — Men
- **Hair:** Natural textures appear frequently and without over-styling — short-cropped fades, locs (both long and shoulder-length), natural Afro texture at various lengths, and loosely textured medium-length European hair. Hair is rarely slick or formally styled; it looks like it was just left to do what it does.
- **Facial hair:** Minimal. Clean-shaven or very light stubble is the norm. No full beards. One or two subjects have a few days of light growth that reads as forgetfulness rather than grooming choice.
- **Accessories:** Caps are the primary grooming supplement — they finish a face rather than frame it. Sunglasses are common but worn pushed down or held.

### Grooming — Women
- **Hair:** Diverse — long natural curls, tight coils, straight blow-dried, tousled unstyled waves, loosely pulled back. No overly styled or formal hair; the dominant quality is healthy and unforced. Colour ranges from natural dark brown and black to lighter blonde tones.
- **Makeup:** Minimal to none visible. The look reads as either bare skin or very light coverage. No heavy contouring, no bold lip, no evening makeup — the skin-forward approach reinforces the effortless quality.

### Energy and Demeanour
- **Grounded confidence:** Subjects carry themselves as though they are entirely comfortable — not performing cool, just being themselves in a context they belong to. The body language is physically open and undefended.
- **Social ease:** Where subjects are together, they are genuinely engaged with each other — mid-conversation, mid-laugh, gesturing as they talk. The camera is sometimes irrelevant to them.

- **Direct gaze:** Confident, slightly flat in affect, unflinching — not smiling, but not cold. Used in solo portraits and some group editorial shots.

- **Rare full smile to camera:** The full warm smile to camera exists (sunny terrace coffee image, train platform couple) but is not the default; it reads as a moment caught, not a pose requested.

### Overall Casting Aesthetic
The casting philosophy is: real people who happen to look interesting. Not conventional modelling industry faces — no symmetry perfection, no single beauty ideal. What every subject shares is a quality of self-possession and specificity. They look like they have their own music taste, their own corner cafe, their own circle. The casting leans into cultural specificity over generic attractiveness. The result is a brand that feels like it belongs to a real community rather than an imagined demographic.

## Product Packaging

**Confirmed spec:** 2026-03-04
**Source:** Bear Nordic website codebase (`app/layout.tsx`, `app/page.tsx`) — packaging inherits the visual language of the website's first/hero section.

### Packet Form and Format

The Bear Nordic condom packet is a **square-format foil or card packet**, consistent with standard individual condom packaging dimensions (approximately 55 × 55 mm). The square format is intentional — it feels considered and premium against the rectangular-default of most competing products. The packet is slim and flat, with no excessive border radius — clean 90-degree corners or very slightly softened edges.

---

### Background Color

- **Primary color:** `#302621` — a very dark warm brown, closer to espresso or charred walnut than to black. It reads nearly black in low light but reveals its warm brown undertone in direct or warm-raking light. It is the exact color used as the Bear Nordic website background and the primary brand ground throughout all digital surfaces.
- **Surface finish:** Matte. No gloss, no metallic finish on the main face. The dark ground absorbs light rather than reflecting it — this is a deliberate premium quality signal. A subtle texture (fine paper grain or matte foil grain) may be visible at close range.
- **Contrast with environment:** On warm clay court surfaces, cognac leather, or natural wood, the `#302621` packet reads as a near-black dark square — a clean, deliberate object that does not compete with the environment.

---

### Typography

- **Typeface:** Mukta Mahee — a clean geometric humanist sans-serif with slightly warm proportions; not a cold Swiss grotesque, not a fashion-editorial display face. Legible, confident, unpretentious.
- **Weights in use:** Regular (400) for body copy and descriptors; Semibold (600) for the brand wordmark; Bold (700) reserved for the single dominant text element if present.
- **Primary text element:** The wordmark **BEAR** set in Mukta Mahee Semibold or Bold, in `#eae4d7` (warm off-white / parchment), tracked wide — approximately 0.3–0.5em letter-spacing. The wordmark is positioned centrally or slightly above center on the packet face. It is the dominant and essentially only visual element on the front face.
- **Secondary text:** A single line of micro-copy — either a size designation (B3 / B4 / B5), a descriptor such as "Ultra-thin" or "Classic", or nothing at all — set in Mukta Mahee Regular at a very small size (equivalent to 7–9pt at physical scale), in `#735a4c` (muted warm brown-grey) or `#7a715c`, tracked at 0.4–0.5em. Positioned below the wordmark with generous vertical space.
- **No other typography:** No straplines, no certifications, no flavor text on the front face. Any regulatory or product information is confined to the reverse or edge of the packet and is not visible in lifestyle photography.

---

### Overall Aesthetic and Design Language

- **Minimal to the point of restraint:** The front face of the packet is essentially one color, one wordmark, and one line of small text. Nothing else. The negative space is the design.
- **Premium through reduction:** The packaging communicates quality through what it removes, not what it adds. There are no gradients, no illustrations, no icons, no decorative borders, no pattern fills.
- **Consistent with website section one:** The packet's visual language is identical to the hero section of the Bear Nordic website — dark `#302621` ground, `#eae4d7` parchment text, wide tracking, generous space, Mukta Mahee. Seeing the packet and the website should feel like seeing the same design system applied at different scales.
- **Color palette of the packet (complete):**
  - Ground: `#302621` (dominant — the entire background)
  - Wordmark: `#eae4d7` (warm off-white)
  - Secondary text: `#735a4c` or `#7a715c` (muted warm brown-grey)
  - No other colors appear on the packet face
- **No logo mark / bear illustration:** The brand identity is purely typographic — the wordmark BEAR is the logo. No bear silhouette, no icon, no emblem.
