**Subject:** Two figures inside a worn boxing gym, each wearing — Full over-the-head bear mask completely encasing the wearer's head: dense dark chocolate brown fur (medium-to-long pile, dishevelled fringe at brow, warm chestnut undertones in light), two small rounded black hard-shell ears set wide on the upper skull, a prominent forward-projecting matte charcoal-grey moulded snout with sculpted nostrils, small dark oval eye openings framed by fur, fixed lower jaw, face plate integrated seamlessly into surrounding fur; mask oversized relative to human head, fur hem resting on shoulders with shirt collar just visible below. Bear paw gloves on both hands: dark chocolate brown fur on dorsal side matching the head, five thick rounded fingers each tipped with a prominent curved black claw approximately 3–5 cm long, palm side featuring a smooth dark burgundy-brown leathery pad with visible circular toe pads; paws significantly oversized relative to human hands, shirt cuff visible as narrow strip at wrist. — Figure one (foreground left): a lean young Black British man, standing side-on to the heavy bag with his weight forward and his full attention on the bag — one bear paw extended mid-punch into the bag's worn leather face, the bag caught at the compression point of impact, swinging back slightly; the other bear paw drawn back and low, ready; dressed in a cream off-white sleeveless jersey vest with a ribbed neckline (the kind found at the back of a gym cupboard, not the front of a shop), dark navy athletic shorts falling to mid-thigh, white high-top boxing boots with thick white laces, white hand-wrap just visible as a narrow strip between paw cuff and sleeve edge. Figure two (mid-ground right): a lean young man of mixed South Asian and White European heritage, seated on a low wooden gym bench facing slightly away from the camera — his bear head turned three-quarters toward figure one and the bag, watching with absorbed ease; both bear paws rest loosely in his lap, a length of off-white cotton hand-wrap draping from between the claws of one paw, mid-unwind — the wrap's loose end falling naturally toward the floor; dressed in an oversized dark forest green cotton T-shirt cut wide at the shoulder and slightly long in the body, washed stone-grey wide-leg athletic trousers tapering at the ankle, white low-profile Adidas Samba sneakers, thick white crew socks pulled to mid-calf. The two figures exist in easy parallel — one entirely in the work, the other in the quiet after it. Neither acknowledges the camera.

**Scene:** Interior of a genuinely used boxing gym in a European city — exposed dark brick walls with patches of worn plaster, a row of heavy leather bags hanging from a low iron ceiling bracket just off-centre in the background, the worn wooden gym floor with its scuff marks and rubber mat edges, a water-stained cork notice board and a round wall-mounted clock at the far wall, a low-hanging cage-shade work light above the bag casting a tight pool of warm tungsten directly onto the action; the space is not curated — it is the product of decades of use. No spectators visible, no coaches, no crowd. Just these two in the gym at an off-peak hour. The scene reinforces Bear Nordic's core ethos: self-possession, preparation, and ease — the quiet confidence of a man who knows what he's doing before he does it.
